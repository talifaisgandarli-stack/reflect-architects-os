# Azerbaijani Language Reference

Native-level Azerbaijani across all registers. Azerbaijani is the state language of Azerbaijan, also spoken in northwestern Iran (Southern Azerbaijan), parts of Georgia, and by diaspora communities. Modern Azerbaijani is in active linguistic evolution — purging Soviet-era Russian loanwords and developing native equivalents. A skilled communicator knows both the old and new vocabulary.

---

## 1. Register spectrum

### Rəsmi (Formal)
Used in: state documents, official correspondence, legal contracts, academic writing, government communication
Characteristics: full grammatical forms, no colloquialisms, titles always used, Azerbaijani-origin vocabulary preferred over Russian loanwords
Example opener: "Hörmətli cənab/xanım [Soyad], Sizə müraciət edərək bildiririk ki..."
Sign-off: "Hörmətlə", "Dərin hörmətlə"

### Biznes / Peşəkar (Business/Professional)
Used in: business emails, proposals, client communication, meetings
Characteristics: warm but structured, titles used on first contact, "Siz" form always, personal warmth is appropriate and expected
Example opener: "Salam [Ad], Ümid edirəm ki, hər şey yaxşıdır..."
Sign-off: "Hörmətlə", "Ehtiramla"

### Yarı-rəsmi (Semi-formal)
Used in: colleague emails, internal communication, professional WhatsApp
Characteristics: "Siz" form with colleagues until close relationship established, contractions appear, warmer tone

### Danışıq (Conversational/Informal)
Used in: friends, family, close colleagues, WhatsApp, social media
Characteristics: "sən" form, contractions, Russian loanwords more acceptable here, Baku dialect features
Example: "Ay bala, nə var nə yox?" / "Nə xəbər?"

### Slang / Gənc dili (Youth/Slang)
Current Baku slang (2024-2025):
- "Yaşa" — cool, great (all-purpose approval)
- "Klass" — classic borrowing from Russian, still widely used
- "Top" — excellent (from English via Russian)
- "Əla" — great (formal origin, now used casually)
- "Nə danışırsan" — "what are you talking about" (disbelief/surprise)
- "Düz deyirsən" — "you're right" (affirmation)
- "Ağlım kəsmir" — "I can't believe it" (lit. "my mind doesn't cut")
- "Dəli işdir" — "it's crazy" (lit. "it's mad work")
- "Hə bəs" — "yeah of course" / "obviously"
- "Canım" — term of endearment, very common

## 2. The "Siz" vs "sən" distinction

This is the most important register marker in Azerbaijani.

**"Siz" (formal/respectful "you"):**
- Always use with: strangers, elders, clients, officials, senior colleagues, anyone you've just met
- Always use in: formal documents, professional emails, official settings
- Using "sən" too early is disrespectful — a serious social error

**"sən" (informal "you"):**
- Use with: close friends, family, children, very close colleagues after established relationship
- Never use in: professional first contact, formal documents, with elders you don't know well

## 3. Russian loanwords: old vs modern Azerbaijani

Soviet-era Azerbaijani absorbed massive Russian vocabulary. Modern standard Azerbaijani is actively replacing these with native or Arabic/Persian-origin equivalents. Context determines which is appropriate.

| Russian loanword (still used informally) | Modern Azerbaijani equivalent | Meaning |
|---|---|---|
| telefon | telefon (same) | telephone |
| plan | plan / niyyət | plan |
| şef | rəis / müdir | boss/chief |
| müşavirə → совещание | iclas / müşavirə | meeting |
| spravka | arayış | reference document |
| komanda | komanda / heyət | team |
| otçot | hesabat | report |
| prokat | icarə | rent/lease |
| mebel | mebel (same) | furniture |
| zavod | zavod / müəssisə | factory |

**Rule:** In formal/written Azerbaijani, always prefer the native equivalent. In casual speech, Russian loanwords are widely used and not marked as errors.

## 4. Industry registers

### Hüquqi (Legal)
Key terms: "müqavilə" (contract), "öhdəlik" (obligation), "hüquq" (right/law), "iddia" (claim), "məhkəmə" (court), "icra" (execution/enforcement), "girov" (collateral), "zəmanət" (guarantee), "kompensasiya" (compensation), "ləğv etmək" (to annul/cancel)
Register: highly formal, Azerbaijani-origin vocabulary strongly preferred, passive constructions common

### Tikinti / Memarlıq (Construction/Architecture)
Key terms: "layihə" (project), "çertyoj" / "plan" (drawing/plan), "smeta" (estimate — Russian loanword still dominant), "podratçı" (contractor), "subpodratçı" (subcontractor), "sifarişçi" (client/ordering party), "texniki nəzarət" (technical supervision), "istismar aktı" (commissioning act), "faza" (phase)
Register: technical Russian loanwords still dominate in construction; formal Azerbaijani for documents

### Maliyyə (Finance)
Key terms: "gəlir" (income/revenue), "xərc" (expense), "mənfəət" (profit), "zərər" (loss), "vergi" (tax), "ƏDV" (VAT — Əlavə Dəyər Vergisi), "balans" (balance), "büdcə" (budget), "ödəniş" (payment), "faktura" (invoice), "debitor borclar" (receivables), "kredit" (loan/credit)

### HR / İnsan Resursları
Key terms: "işçi" (employee), "maaş" (salary), "məzuniyyət" (leave/vacation), "performans" (performance), "attestasiya" (certification/evaluation), "karyera" (career), "vəzifə" (position), "ştat" (staff/headcount), "iş təcrübəsi" (work experience)

### İT / Texnologiya
Key terms: "proqram təminatı" (software), "sistem" (system), "istifadəçi" (user), "məlumat bazası" (database), "şifrə" (password), "interfeys" (interface), "yeniləmə" (update), "tətbiq" (application)

## 5. Email and letter conventions

### Formal letter structure
1. "Hörmətli cənab/xanım [Soyad]," — always family name in formal
2. Body: structured paragraphs, clear purpose stated early
3. "Hörmətlə," / "Dərin hörmətlə," + Full name + Title + Organization

### Business email structure
1. "Salam [Ad]," — first name acceptable in business if relationship established
2. "Ümid edirəm ki, hər şey yaxşıdır" — standard opener (optional but common)
3. Body: clear, warm, structured
4. "Hörmətlə" / "Ehtiramla" + Name

### WhatsApp / informal
- "Salam!" / "Hər şey yaxşıdır?" — standard casual opener
- No strict structure needed
- Emoji widely accepted in informal business WhatsApp in Azerbaijan

## 6. Cultural communication norms

**Relationship first.** Azerbaijani business culture is high-context. Ask about health, family (generally), and wellbeing before getting to business — especially in person or on first emails.

**Respect for elders and hierarchy.** Always use "Siz" with anyone senior. Address titles: "müəllim" (teacher/doctor, general respect), "cənab" (Mr./Sir), "xanım" (Ms./Madam).

**Warmth is professional.** A warm, personal tone in business email is not unprofessional — it's expected. Cold, purely transactional communication reads as rude.

**"Xeyir işə" / "Uğurlar"** — common well-wishes. "Uğurlar" (good luck/success) is appropriate in most contexts. "Allah köməyin olsun" (May God help you) is common and not considered overly religious — it's a standard expression.

**Refusals are indirect.** A direct "no" is often softened: "Bu məsələyə baxacağıq" (We'll look into this matter) often means no. "İmkan daxilində" (within possibilities) signals uncertainty/reluctance.

## 7. Common errors non-natives make in Azerbaijani

- Using "sən" too early with strangers — always start with "Siz"
- Russian word order (verb before object) — Azerbaijani is SOV (Subject-Object-Verb)
- Overusing Russian loanwords in formal contexts
- Missing case suffixes (Azerbaijani has 6 grammatical cases — vocative, nominative, genitive, dative, accusative, ablative, locative)
- Confusing "var" (there is/exists) and "olan" (which exists/that has)
- Not matching vowel harmony in suffixes (Azerbaijani vowel harmony is consistent and required)
