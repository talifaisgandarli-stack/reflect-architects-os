# Russian Language Reference

Native-level Russian across all registers. Russian is spoken natively across the former Soviet space — including as a major business and social language in Azerbaijan. Russian has distinct regional flavours (Moscow, St. Petersburg, Kyiv/Ukrainian Russian, Baku Russian) and highly structured formal conventions that differ markedly from conversational norms.

---

## 1. Register spectrum

### Официальный (Official/Formal)
Used in: state documents, official letters, legal contracts, formal reports
Characteristics: full grammatical forms, impersonal constructions ("Настоящим сообщается, что..."), no colloquialisms, specific epistolary formulas
Example opener: "Уважаемый господин/госпожа [Фамилия],"
Sign-off: "С уважением," / "С глубоким уважением,"

### Деловой (Business)
Used in: business emails, proposals, professional correspondence
Characteristics: clear, structured, formal "Вы" (capital V), titles used, warmer than official but structured
Example opener: "Добрый день, [Имя Отчество],"
Sign-off: "С уважением," + Full name + Title

### Нейтральный (Neutral)
Used in: articles, documentation, editorial, general communication
Characteristics: standard literary Russian, no marked formality either way

### Разговорный (Conversational)
Used in: colleague communication, casual emails, informal meetings
Characteristics: "ты" form with established colleagues, contractions, colloquialisms
Example: "Привет! Как дела? Можешь прислать файл?"

### Сленг / Молодёжный (Slang/Youth)
Current Russian slang (2024-2025):
- "Красава" — well done / good job (от "красавчик")
- "Норм" — fine/okay (от "нормально")
- "Жиза" — truth/relatable (от "жизнь")
- "Кайф" — pleasure/great (widely used across generations now)
- "Зашквар" — embarrassing/cringe
- "Агонь" / "Огонь" — fire/amazing (lit. "fire")
- "Лол" — lol (direct borrowing)
- "Чилить" — to chill/relax
- "Хайп" — hype
- "Топ" — top/excellent
- "Краш" — crush (romantic interest)
- "Рофл" — laugh/joke (от ROFL)
- "Бро" — bro
- "Ок бумер" — ok boomer
- Dated: "Превед медвед", "Фтопку"

## 2. The "Вы" vs "ты" distinction

**"Вы" (formal/respectful "you" — capital V):**
- Always use with: strangers, clients, senior colleagues, anyone in official capacity, elders
- Always use in: formal documents, business emails, first professional contact
- Switching to "ты" too early is presumptuous — wait for the other person to initiate

**"ты" (informal "you"):**
- Use with: close friends, family, children, close long-standing colleagues
- In Russian business culture, many colleagues stay on "Вы" indefinitely even after years of working together
- In startups and tech companies, "ты" culture is more common from the start

---

## 3. Patronymic (Отчество) usage

A key feature of Russian formality. In formal/professional settings, people are addressed as:
- **Имя + Отчество** (First name + Patronymic): "Александр Николаевич", "Наталья Ивановна"
- Never just by surname in direct address (that's more Soviet/military)
- In modern business, first name only is increasingly accepted, especially in tech/startups
- When in doubt, use Имя + Отчество — it's never wrong to be more respectful

## 4. Industry registers

### Юридический (Legal)
Key terms: "договор" (contract), "обязательство" (obligation), "право" (right/law), "иск" (claim/lawsuit), "суд" (court), "исполнение" (execution/performance), "залог" (collateral), "гарантия" (guarantee), "компенсация" (compensation), "расторжение" (termination), "стороны" (the parties)
Register: highly formal, impersonal constructions, specific legal formulas

### Строительство / Архитектура (Construction/Architecture)
Key terms: "проект" (project), "чертёж" (drawing), "смета" (estimate/BOQ), "подрядчик" (contractor), "субподрядчик" (subcontractor), "заказчик" (client/ordering party), "технадзор" (technical supervision), "акт приёмки" (acceptance act), "этап" (phase/stage), "объект" (facility/site)
Register: technical, Russian construction terminology still dominant in post-Soviet countries

### Финансы (Finance)
Key terms: "доход" (income), "расход" (expense), "прибыль" (profit), "убыток" (loss), "налог" (tax), "НДС" (VAT — Налог на добавленную стоимость), "баланс" (balance), "бюджет" (budget), "оплата" (payment), "счёт-фактура" (invoice), "дебиторская задолженность" (receivables), "кредит" (loan/credit)

### IT / Технологии
Key terms: "программное обеспечение" (software), "разработка" (development), "пользователь" (user), "база данных" (database), "интерфейс" (interface), "обновление" (update), "приложение" (application), "бэкенд/фронтенд" (backend/frontend — direct borrowings)
Register: Russian IT culture mixes native terms with English borrowings freely

### HR / Кадры
Key terms: "сотрудник" (employee), "зарплата" (salary), "отпуск" (vacation/leave), "аттестация" (performance evaluation), "карьера" (career), "должность" (position), "штатное расписание" (staffing schedule), "трудовой договор" (employment contract), "увольнение" (dismissal)

## 5. Email conventions

### Formal letter structure
1. "Уважаемый/ая [Имя Отчество]," (Dear [First name Patronymic],)
2. "Настоящим письмом..." / "В связи с..." — formal opener
3. Body: structured, clear purpose, formal constructions
4. "С уважением," + Full name + Title + Organization + Contact

### Business email structure
1. "Добрый день, [Имя]!" or "Здравствуйте, [Имя Отчество]!"
2. Body: direct, clear, structured paragraphs
3. "С уважением," + Name

### Casual/colleague
1. "Привет!" / "Добрый день!"
2. Direct and brief
3. "Спасибо" / "Удачи" / "До связи"

## 6. Regional differences relevant to Azerbaijan

### Baku Russian
- Azerbaijani speakers' Russian often features: SOV word order influence, dropped articles (Russian has none, but sentence structure may feel different), Azerbaijani loanwords ("ay bala", "vallah"), warmer social register than Moscow Russian
- "Слушай" (listen/hey) as universal conversation opener
- Mix of formal/informal that feels natural in Baku context

### Moscow vs St. Petersburg
- Moscow: more direct, business-focused, status-conscious
- St. Petersburg: more literary, slightly more formal, "intelligentsia" register

## 7. Cultural communication norms

**Direct but not rude.** Russian professional communication is more direct than Arabic or Azerbaijani, less warm in openers. Getting to the point quickly is normal and not disrespectful.

**Pessimism is a feature.** Russian communication often acknowledges difficulties upfront: "Это непросто, но..." (This isn't easy, but...). Excessive optimism reads as naive or insincere.

**Compliments in business.** Less effusive than American English. "Хорошая работа" (Good work) is a strong compliment — Russians don't over-praise.

**"Нет" is more direct.** Russians say no more directly than Azerbaijanis or Arabs. "Это невозможно" (That's impossible) means no. You don't need to soften it as much.

**Hospitality language.** "Угощайтесь" (help yourself), "Не стесняйтесь" (don't be shy/please feel free) are standard warmth markers even in professional settings.

## 8. Common errors non-natives make in Russian

- Wrong aspect (perfective vs imperfective verbs) — this is the hardest feature for non-natives
- Wrong case (Russian has 6 grammatical cases — nominative, genitive, dative, accusative, instrumental, prepositional)
- Gender agreement errors on adjectives
- Missing soft sign (ь) in verb forms
- Using "Вы" without capital in formal writing — always capitalize in Russian
- Overusing "очень" (very) — sounds weak; use more precise intensifiers
- "Я хочу вам сказать" sounds slightly aggressive; soften to "Хотел(а) бы поделиться"
